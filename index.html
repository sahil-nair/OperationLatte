Because Every Voice Matters

Sahil & Naba for Georgetown

What We Stand For

1. Inclusivity: We want to amplify voices and bring everyone to the table.

  • You can’t have a student government that’s insular and ignores most of campus
  • Everyone can gain something from working with student government
  • We hear you, and we want to bring you to the table

2. Accountability: Georgetown can, and should, be better — and that hinges on student government

  • There are too many barriers to achievement at Georgetown
  • There’s a lack of adequate support, depriving us of a sturdy foundation to start from
  • Every person & organization can benefit from GUSA; let’s make it work for everyone

3. Opportunity: We will help your ideas come to fruition.

  • Georgetown students are brilliant and full of amazing ideas
  • Georgetown should be an incubator for innovation and entrepreneurship
  • GUSA has many tools at its disposal, and we want to use them to work for you

This is Our Vision

There’s a fairly standard image of achievement at Georgetown: a dozen clubs, perfect grades, and a Hillternship. But the truth is that everyone has their own definition of what it means to achieve, whether its a creative start-up, a photography passion-project, an individual research project, or starting a new club.

Unfortunately, Georgetown often poses far more barriers to achieving in unconventional ways — and we want to change that.

We believe student government can remove barriers to success for every person and every organization — helping you achieve according to your own definition. We want to make GUSA work for all of us.

Why You Should Vote For Us

We're in this together.

  • The two of us go back five years to our days in high school speech and debate
  • We’re natural partners and best friends — the closer, the better

We aren’t your typical GUSA candidates.

  • GUSA today is run by and for a small group of students at Georgetown
  • We’re deeply experienced in Georgetown leadership — delivering outside ideas & outside voices

But this isn’t about the two of us; it’s about all of us

  • Everyone at Georgetown has their own story — and we want to hear your stories
  • Together, with all voices at the table, we can restore legitimacy and trust in student government

Let’s write the next chapter of our time at Georgetown together — because every voice matters.
