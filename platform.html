Our Policy Goals
Academic Affairs
  • Reexamining the University academic calendar in order to best support the health and success of all students such as:
    • potential increase in study days
    • creation of midterm days
    • freshmen year course forgiveness
    • additional Thanksgiving break day
    • early move-in coming back from Winter Break
  • Encourage more cross-listing across schools and disciplines to diversify student experiences and encourage holistic education
  • Partner with University Deans to make minors to be accessible across schools in order to give students credit for the courses they take.
  • Dialoguing with student advocates to promote a greater diversity of course listings, including supporting the establishment of a Women and Gender Studies Department, and Latinx and Asian American studies programs
  • Facilitate relationships between Academic departments and student organizations in order to amplify student voices and create faculty-student partnerships for University initiatives

“A lot of academic changes feel like small steps that make a big difference in your future.”
-Sonali Mirpuri ‘20

Accessibility
  • Push administration to create more accessible routes across campus and make the well-being of our students with disabilities a priority through actions, not just words
  • Create a fund that can be used by campus clubs for their members’ accessibility needs, for example, if clubs need to rent ramps in order to hold events in normally inaccessible locations.
  • Implement signage across campus pointing students and visitors toward accessible entrances and elevator locations which are otherwise difficult to find.
  • Lobby for increased funding for the ARC.
  • Increase the amount of ADA buttons on doors, so that students with disabilities have equal access to campus locations. Specifically, we’d like to start by adding buttons to:
    • Common rooms in dorms across campus;
    • At least one accessible bathroom in every building across campus;
    • Laundry rooms across campus;
    • Epicurean;
    • GU Politics office (both entrances);
    • GSP office;
    • Healy entrance under the cannons;
    • SFS Deans’ Offices;
    • College Deans’ Offices;
    • Academic Department offices in ICC;
    • Entrances/exits on Leavey Esplanade.
  • In the long-term, widen sidewalks for greater ease of access for wheelchair-users on campus.
  • Fix potholes and loose bricks on campus pathways, which present a safety issue for students on campus regardless of their mobility needs.
  • Increase the number of doors connected to the ARC’s system of remote control door-openers used by students with mobility issues.
  • Replace the three-part black trash bins in outdoor locations across campus with models that can be used by students with limited arm strength
  • Make well-needed updates to the campus accessibility map created by GUSA, which as of now does not include new buildings like Arrupe.
  • Launching an inter-campus shuttle system in order to make Advocate for a university-wide audit of ramps, buttons, and elevators
  • Create an information series so students can recognize ADA compliance violations
  • Improve the request system for mobility aids for people who are visiting campus
  • Work with UIS to ensure that club websites and other official websites of the university meet website content accessibility guidelines

“This came to mind when I joined GGC and they were going to spend money from their own budget to rent a ramp from a local company so that I could attend a club event. Likewise, events held at Georgetown houses like Casa Latina are impossible for me to attend.”
-Anna Landre, 21’

Affordability
  • Initiating steps to move towards a more reasonable cost of living, including exploring the possibility of smaller meal plans in the future, grocery discount options or a more open dining plan
  • Recognizing the overarching importance of affordable campus living, establishing a stand-alone affordability task force to examine cross-cutting opportunities to reduce student expenses and offer a more equitable living experience at Georgetown
Arts
  • Bringing new voices into the planning of Arts Week to build on the successes of the past and to incorporate a diversity of student work across mediums, disciplines, subjects and styles
  • Collaborating with student artists and student art groups to feature the creativity of Georgetown students in tangible ways across campus

"I didn't want the arts to make me well-rounded and get me into Georgetown, then stop being a part of my life once I got here. The many groups, classes, and museums that have allowed me to continue having a creative outlet have made me a happier, healthier Hoya, and I would love to see them become more accessible and encouraged to all.”
-Genevieve Pool

Athletics
  • Support renovation plans to Kehoe Field both in the short term and long term
  • Protecting access to alternative practice locations for all of Georgetown’s athletics groups while Kehoe Field remains out of commission
  • Build advocacy partnership between GUSA and our student athletes to support affordability, access to resources and academic flexibility
Club Culture
  • Elevate the voices of marginalized clubs and student voices across campus through GUSA support for forums and campus wide dialogue so that they can stand alongside larger, better known organizations
  • With the institution of Community Advisory Groups, facilitate discussions on access, affordability, inclusion and opportunity among the different student organizations at Georgetown, and connect those efforts directly to the GUSA advocacy mission
  • Implement forums and increase access to faculty members for campus wide dialogue for inclusivity within student organizations
  • Racial sensitivity and minority experience training and dialogue within organizations with over 30 people

“One of the reasons I want Naba and Sahil to win is because I think they reflect Georgetown where it counts - both in policy and people. Their policies go hand in hand with their commitment to empowering and listening to the voices of underrepresented communities here on campus. And the truth is that for many people, policy matters, but so does representation in leadership. I would encourage everyone to give Naba and Sahil a shot and listen to their vision for Georgetown in the coming days.”

Dining
  • Creating options for smaller and more cost-effective meal plans for freshmen and sophomores in order to minimize waste or excessive spending
  • Establishing partnerships with local food providers to move towards a more open dining plan format
  • Dialoguing with Aramark and Leo’s administrators to increase transparency of the institution’s waste management practices, and advocating for sustainable practices at all campus dining locations
Entrepreneurship
  • Creating networks with both local businesses and on-campus student organizations to connect GUSA Fund recipients, as well as those of other student entrepreneurial initiatives, with incubation consulting services
  • Reinforcing GUSA Fund as a student advocacy mechanism by funding projects that aim to address four pilot issue areas, in an effort to collaborate as a student body on these topics which serve as universally cross-cutting themes impacting the majority of campus
  • Partnering with the Maker Hub to make its resources more accessible to Georgetown students
  • Increase institutional support for student for-profit ventures, by offering access to resources and funding for business development
  • Connect student entrepreneurial initiatives with campus support, such as student consulting groups, to provide a workshop space to cultivate and grow new innovative projects
Federal Relations

  • Collaborate with on-campus student groups to identify new and relevant issue areas which FedRel and GUSA can begin advocacy programs for
Free Speech

  • Supporting club liaisons (both recognized and not recognized) to amplify those voices through the executive, particularly in GUSA initiatives such as the Community Advisory Groups
Greek Life

  • Partnering with the Council of Advisory Boards to encourage all Greek Life organizations to know and take advantage of opportunities they have to promote their communities
LGBTQ+ Advocacy
  • Build on the progress of the previous administration in achieving an LLC for Gender and Sexuality, by continuing to identify and push for inclusive spaces in every corner of campus
  • Continue work alongside administrators and active student groups in ensuring that all campus spaces suit the needs of all students at Georgetown, particularly in the areas of housing and bathroom accessibility
  • Promote resources for LGBTQ+ students on campus through GUSA channels, such as distinguished all-gender bathrooms and those resources provide by the LGBTQ Center
  • Gender neutral bathrooms on every floor and a safe living space for LGBTQ identifying people (like a Casa Latina, Black House, etc..)
  • LGBTQ sensitivity training at the Student Health Center
Master Planning & Community Engagement
  • Continue to take student input on matters which impact the campus community at-large, specifically the University Administration’s timeline for instituting a ban on smoking on campus
  • Active participation and inclusion of representative student voices in the University Master Planning Consortium
  • Advocating clear and actionable priorities on behalf of the student body when it comes to future campus projects
  • Wide GUSA participation in community events, including the Annual Georgetown Community Day in the Fall, as well as through membership in the local Advisory Neighborhood Council and Georgetown Community Partnership
Mental Health
  • Continuing support to institutionalize and expand the off-campus mental health resource stipend by asking alumni groups to contribute
  • Instituting a special entity within GUSA for the purpose of both advancing on and off campus mental health resources, and working to break the stigma of mental health services
  • Reinforcing GUSA support for the incredible work being done in this area by numerous student groups and campus partners
  • Launching a behavioral health and accessibility review board to ensure that the University is complying with regulations that protect the rights of students with disabilities (distinct from student health efforts which focus on care, not discrimination
  • Expanding the CAPS facility and supporting shifting the facility to the front of the building to a more accessible entrance with less stigma
  • Launching one-credit course through the ARC, HES, and Yates that educates students on living a balanced life through positive psychology
  • Creating an infographic that explains the four health entities (SHC, ARC, CAPS, HES). Install in all bathroom stalls - this gives students the opportunity to see this information in an anonymous setting (and potentially realize they should go even if they hadn’t been thinking about it - which is necessary to visit the website

Professional Development
  • Working with the Career Center to bring diverse recruiters in fields like liberal arts, humanities, and education to serve Georgetown students from all academic backgrounds.
  • Create career fair opportunities for students to get exposed to career options based on different backgrounds. We want to include the circumstances of different majors, international students, different schools, and backgrounds.
  • Establishing a student mentor program to connect students in similar career fields in order to navigate the process.

"While our experiences on the Hilltop are defined by far more than the jobs and internships we end up pursuing, pre-professional development is important for obvious reasons. Racial & Cultural Inclusitivity
  • Establish campus Community Advisory Groups, collections of student clubs and groups organized by issue area of interest, in an effort to include new voices and perspectives into the student government process and to provide an effective platform for raising issues which GUSA may want to become involved in
  • New programs through partnership between GUSA and the Career Center cultivated to highlighting opportunities for the diversity of student backgrounds and interests here on campus
  • Continuously being aware of opportunities to create GUSA-student special task force partnerships to address issues of representation, accessibility or inclusion on campus and building clear mandates for how the student body should move forward to address those issues as they arise
  • Launching a comprehensive discrimination climate survey
Religious Groups
  • Continue to promote spiritual retreat opportunities through the Campus Ministry department for students regardless of faith background
  • Commit to providing both a space for dialogue and a force for advocacy and action in cases of intolerance or bigotry
Residential Living
  • Grow transparency in the administrative timeline for residential space renovation plans
  • Create a space for popular student input into the residential hall renovation timeline, addressing both short term needs and long term vision for the campus
  • Simplify and streamline the process for facilities work requests, and publicize alternatives to specific common problems faced on campus to reduce traffic on the work requests site
  • Supporting the great new resource of Hoya House Hunters for students interested in living off-campus as an upperclassmen; designing targeted publicity campaigns to maximize the success of this new program
Sexual Assault Prevention & Response
  • Bringing “Know Your IX” trainings to Georgetown as part of sexual assault awareness particularly for student group leaders
  • Amplifying the missions and programs offered by SAPE (Sexual Assault Peer Educators) and resources provided by the Student Health Center
  • Mandating all GUSA Executive officials receive training on sexual assault prevention and bystander strategies upon joining the administration

“When ⅓ of undergraduates at the university have been sexually assaulted, we Hoyas know we need to do better. The university has stepped up in the wake of federal sexual assault regulation modifications but change truly starts with us students.”
-Anusha Agarwal, SFS ‘21

Socioeconomic Advocacy
  • Support the outstanding work of the previous administration in their establishment of the GUSA Food Pantry, by growing awareness and flow of resources for this useful program
  • Connect students to affordability resources both within the GUSA family and at the University level
  • Offer chances for students to dialogue with the GUSA Executive on new ways we can serve as advocates for the needs of all students at Georgetown
Student Conduct
  • Serving as an advocate and liaison particularly for students living off campus as they navigate both University and city disciplinary actions
  • Increasing clarity for students, especially freshmen, on what may constitute a violation during Health and Safety Inspections, including through GUSA-led awareness campaigns
  • Working to increase transparency into University policies on conduct violation and subsequent punishment options
  • Support the work of the Student Advocacy Office in assisting all students with knowing their rights in the instance of a conduct violation and protecting their due process

“One time when I was written up for a noise violation, I talked to my friend who was involved with SAO and he was a huge help in talking me through how that process worked. The Office is a great resource for students, and I hope that GUSA does all it can to promote that resource for everyone on campus.”

Student Health & Wellness
  • Advocating for weekly STI screenings offered here on campus for students
  • Incorporating a Healthy Sexual Education and Behavior seminar into New Student Orientation
  • Partnering with NHS and other campus partners to bring increased awareness to annual flu shots at Yates
  • Working with Facilities to ensure that all water filters across campus are monitored for quality and replaced in a timely manner
  • Reexamining the mandated health information sharing components of the student health insurance plan, in an effort to protect student privacy
  • Piloting new ways to manage wait times at the Student Health Center and/or Medstar, through online check-in platforms

Student Orgs

Sustainability

Technology

Transfer Student Affairs

Undocumented Student Rights

Unrecognized Student Group Affairs
